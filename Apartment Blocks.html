What Qualifies as an Apartment Building?

One common question we get from sellers is, “Does my property qualify as an apartment building?” While there are many types of multifamily properties, most of them fall under the broad definition of an “apartment building.” This term generally refers to any larger structure divided into separate residences. These buildings may go by various names depending on the region, such as “apartment block,” “apartment complex,” or “condominiums.”

The Evolution of Apartment Buildings

The concept of apartment living dates back to ancient Rome, where multi-family buildings known as “insula” housed large numbers of residents. During the industrial revolution, apartment blocks became common in cities, providing affordable housing for the growing workforce. Over time, apartment living has evolved to offer more comfort, style, and convenience, meeting the needs of diverse populations in various settings.
Today’s apartment buildings range from low-rise suburban buildings to high-rise urban towers, and even mixed-use complexes with residential units above retail spaces. Each type serves a specific demographic and meets distinct needs, whether in a bustling city center or a quiet suburban area.

Types of Apartment Buildings

Low-Rise Apartments

These smaller buildings, typically 2-4 stories high, are often found in suburban areas where they blend well with single-family homes. They may house anywhere from four to eight units or more.

High-Rise Apartments

Found primarily in urban centers, high-rise apartment buildings can soar over 30 stories, accommodating hundreds of units. These structures are ideal for high-density areas and often come with advanced amenities and conveniences.

Mixed-Use Apartments

Common in downtown areas, mixed-use buildings combine residential units with commercial spaces on the ground floor. This setup appeals to residents who enjoy the convenience of nearby shops, restaurants, and services.

Modern Construction and Amenities

AApartment building construction has evolved significantly, with modern materials like energy-efficient windows, sustainable timber, and recycled materials joining classic brick and concrete. This variety ensures better safety, energy efficiency, and reduced environmental impact. At higher income levels, apartment complexes often feature amenities such as pools, fitness centers, package lockers, and community spaces, adding value to the property.

Options for Selling Your Apartment Building

Make Renovations and Improvements

If your goal is to get the highest possible price, making updates to the property can increase its appeal and value. Adding modern features like smart technology, granite countertops, or energy-efficient appliances can boost the property’s market value. However, this approach requires a significant investment of time and money, which may be difficult if you’re already looking to exit the investment.

Sell “As-Is”

Alternatively, you can list the property as-is, appealing to buyers looking for renovation projects. This approach may take longer, as you’ll likely encounter lower offers initially, but it can save you from having to make costly repairs. If you’re looking for a faster, hassle-free sale, GIGI Buys Homes can purchase your property as-is, allowing you to avoid the cleaning, renovations, and waiting that typically come with traditional sales.
